All you need to know about ECF

Equity crowdfunding is an innovative approach to investing that's transforming the future of finance. For investors, ECF provides an avenue for the public, namely the retail investors to invest in deals that were previously accessible to the select few. In return, they acquire equity stakes in private businesses and could potentially earn a positive return on their investments. With the advent of ECF, retail investors can now invest alongside angel and sophisticated investors.

One of the key benefits of equity crowdfunding is that it democratizes the world of investing by giving retail investors access to high-growth private businesses and pre-IPO startups, which were previously only available to a selected group of investors. Be a Smart Investor

The public needs to be a smart investor. Smart investors:

  1. diversify their money into different investment products
  2. understand what they invest in, and
  3. don't invest with companies or individuals that are not licensed or approved by the Securities Commission (SC).

Investors should be aware of scams from unauthorized individuals or companies. The SC has an Investor Alert List which is regularly updated. The list includes unauthorized websites, investment products, individuals, and companies that investors should be cautious about. It helps investors spot fraudulent investments.

  1. Investor alert list
  2. International Organisation of Securities Commissions (IOSCO) list

The International Organisation of Securities Commissions (IOSCO) is an international body of the world's securities regulators. IOSCO also publishes alerts and warnings from securities regulators in relation to unauthorized persons or entities that do not comply with securities laws of the respective jurisdictions (firms which are not authorized to provide investment services in the jurisdiction which issued the alert or warning).
